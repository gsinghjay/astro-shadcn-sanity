## Page: About the Capstone Program (/about-the-capstone-program/)

### Hero

Subtitle: "Bridging Academic Excellence with Industry Innovation"

### Welcome

The YWCC Capstone Program is the flagship industry-academic partnership at NJIT's Ying Wu College of Computing. Each semester, students collaborate with real companies, nonprofits, startups, and research groups to build solutions that address real-world problems. Our goal is to help students develop into confident, work-ready professionals while providing industry partners with access to new talent, fresh ideas, and meaningful innovation.

### Mission

To create meaningful partnerships between ambitious students and forward-thinking organizations. Through collaboration, we foster innovation while preparing the next generation of technology leaders for successful careers in an evolving digital landscape.

### Core Values

We believe in a culture that is modern, relevant, and driven by purpose. Our program empowers students to think creatively, learn independently, and build with confidence. We value curiosity, innovation, integrity, and the courage to take on real-world challenges.

### What Is the YWCC Capstone Program?

The Capstone Program is the Ying Wu College of Computing's signature experiential learning initiative. Each semester, student teams partner with industry sponsors to design, develop, and deliver real solutions to real problems. These projects mirror professional environments -- complete with teamwork, project planning, research, prototyping, and technical execution. Sponsors gain access to fresh perspectives, emerging talent, and innovative solutions. Students gain hands-on experience, mentorship, leadership opportunities, and a portfolio-worthy project.

### How the Capstone Experience Works (4 steps)

1. **Problem Exploration:** Teams meet with sponsors to understand the challenge, analyze requirements, and research similar solutions. Requirements expected to change.
2. **Planning & Design:** Students prepare workflows, system designs, timelines, and prototypes. It's here where the project idea is refined and the process is finalized.
3. **Development & Testing:** Teams build the solution using industry-standard tools while refining it through feedback cycles. All while communicating with their sponsors.
4. **Final Delivery & Presentation:** Sponsors receive a working prototype or final product, along with documentation. The semester concludes with a showcase where teams present their outcomes.

### Why the Capstone Program Matters (6 benefit cards)

1. **Industry Collaboration** -- Work directly with student teams throughout the semester to explore real challenges, refine requirements, and guide project outcomes.
2. **Innovation & Exploration** -- Test new ideas and technologies in a low-risk academic environment while gaining fresh perspectives and creative solutions.
3. **Talent Pipeline** -- Engage with skilled students who have already demonstrated their technical abilities, teamwork, and problem-solving skills.
4. **Research & Development** -- Accelerate internal research and development efforts by leveraging student-driven analysis, prototyping, and implementation.
5. **Ongoing Engagement** -- Receive bi-weekly updates, progress reviews, and open communication with a dedicated project team.
6. **Community Impact** -- Give back to the tech community by mentoring emerging professionals while helping shape the next generation of industry talent.

## Page: Research & Development (/research-development/)

### Hero

Headline: "Elevate Your Digital Future"

Body: The YWCC Capstone Program is a hands-on, industry-focused experience where student teams partner with sponsors to explore real research, development, and innovation challenges. Each semester begins with the Capstone Industry Open House, where sponsors present project ideas and recruit their teams. Once matched, students create a one-page project scope that outlines objectives, risks, deliverables, and expected outcomes -- establishing a shared understanding between the team, sponsor, and faculty.

Using Agile, Scrum, and the MDDDE lifecycle (Manage, Define, Design, Develop, Evaluate), students move through a structured development process that mirrors modern industry workflows. They write user stories, plan sprints, build iterative prototypes, and present progress through weekly check-ins, midterm evaluations, and a final showcase. Throughout this journey, sponsors provide guidance, domain expertise, and direction, transforming classroom learning into a real-world collaborative partnership.

### Title: "How to Become a Sponsor - Complete Guide"

### 5.1 Applying for Sponsorship

**Step 1 - Initial Inquiry**
Submit your interest through our sponsorship form or contact our program director.
- Provide company information
- Indicate areas of interest
- Describe your technical challenges

**Step 2 - Consultation Meeting**
Schedule a meeting with our sponsorship team to discuss:
- Your technical challenges and goals
- Available student teams and expertise
- Timeline and expectations
- Sponsorship benefits and commitments

**Step 3 - Project Proposal**
Work with our team to develop a project proposal including:
- Clear problem statement
- Desired outcomes and deliverables
- Technical requirements
- Resource commitments

**Step 4 - Agreement Signing**
Complete the sponsorship process:
- Review and sign sponsorship agreement
- Complete NDA if required
- No monetary cost - sponsorship is free
- Receive sponsor portal access

### 5.2 Student Recruitment Procedures

**Process:**
1. **Project Posting:** Your project is presented to eligible students
2. **Student Applications:** Teams apply based on interest and skills
3. **Skills Assessment:** We evaluate technical competencies
4. **Team Formation:** 4-5 students with complementary skills
5. **Sponsor Review:** You review and approve team selections
6. **Kickoff Meeting:** Initial meeting between sponsor and team

**What You Can Expect:**
- **Timeline:** First two weeks of semester for team assignment
- **Team Size:** 4-5 senior students
- **Skills Mix:** Programming, design, project management, QA
- **Faculty Advisor:** Each team has dedicated faculty guidance

### 5.3 Collaborating with Your Team

**Communication Channels:**
- **Regular Meetings:** Weekly meetings and milestone reviews
- **Collaboration Tools:** Slack, Teams, or your preferred platform
- **Version Control:** GitHub/GitLab access for code review

**Best Practices:**
- Set clear expectations early in the project
- Provide timely feedback on deliverables
- Be available for questions and clarifications
- Respect academic schedules (exams, holidays)
- Encourage innovation and creative problem-solving

### 5.4 Your Expected Contributions

**1. General Expectations**
- Dedicate 1-2 hours per week for team interaction
- Provide domain expertise and industry context
- Review and approve project milestones
- Facilitate access to necessary resources/data

**2. Acknowledgment Agreement**
- Recognize student contributions in any publications
- Allow use of project in student portfolios
- Provide recommendation letters for exceptional students
- Participate in capstone showcase events

**3. Nondisclosure Agreement**
- Standard NDA available if required
- Protects proprietary information
- Covers students, faculty, and university
- Customizable to company requirements

**4. Required Event Attendance**
- Kickoff Meeting: Project initiation (Required)
- Monthly Reviews: Progress evaluation
- Final Presentation: Project showcase (Required)
- Capstone Showcase: Campus Center Ballroom event

**5. Agile/SCRUM Participation**
- Sprint planning sessions (bi-weekly)
- Sprint reviews and feedback
- Backlog prioritization input
- User acceptance testing

**6. Student Evaluation**
- Mid-term performance feedback
- Final evaluation of deliverables
- Individual student assessments
- Input on grading (advisory)

### 5.5 Team's Expected Contributions

**Deliverables:**
- Project charter and timeline
- Technical design documentation
- Working prototype/MVP
- Source code with documentation
- User manuals and guides
- Final presentation and demo
- Project handover materials

**Professional Conduct:**
- Professional communication
- Timely delivery of milestones
- Regular progress updates
- Confidentiality compliance
- Iterative development process
- Full semester commitment
- Quality-focused outcomes

### Timeline & Milestones

13-15 weeks development period. Available semesters: Spring, Fall, Summer.

### FAQs

**Q: What is the typical cost of sponsorship?**
A: There is no monetary cost for sponsorship. The program is free for sponsors. Your primary investment is time (1-2 hours per week) for mentoring and guidance.

**Q: Who owns the intellectual property?**
A: IP arrangements are negotiated between the sponsor and NJIT, with flexibility to meet your needs. Students retain the right to use the project in their portfolios.

**Q: Can we hire students after the project?**
A: Absolutely! Many sponsors use the program as an extended interview process. Companies have hired individual students and even entire teams. Recent examples include Eco-Enterprise hiring 8 students with equity partnerships.

**Q: What if we need to change project scope?**
A: We understand that project requirements can evolve. Scope changes can be discussed with the team and faculty advisor during sprint planning sessions. Major changes may require approval from the program director.
