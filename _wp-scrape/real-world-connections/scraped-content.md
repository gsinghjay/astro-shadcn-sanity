## Welcome to Real World Connections

![](images/home-rwcus.jpg)

Real World Connections (RWC) is a unique program that offers talented middle and high school students the opportunity to be involved in a fun-filled and hands-on educational experience.

Students have a unique chance to work with well-known companies or become entrepreneurs as well as industry subject matter experts, alumni, NJIT students and faculty. RWC is the founder of a “University without Walls” model.

## Current & Upcoming Programs

## About RWC

### Classes

RWC classes work as a very social “learning organization” co-designed by students, university, and industry.

These classes adapt to students’ demands, learn from weekly feedback and respond rapidly to real world clients’ expectations, so education becomes more relevant, exciting and rewarding.

### Projects

In addition to classes, RWC offers hands-on training in project management, leadership, entrepreneurship, research and development, software tools, programming, engineering, social, presentation and communication skills.

This training is integrated with mentorship by industry, university and peers.

## Page: About (/about/)

### About

**Screenshot:** `screenshots/about.png`

![](images/about-image-3.png)

Twenty years ago, Osama Eljabiri, Ph.D. created a college-level capstone program at NJIT that focused on project-based learning and real-world experiences.

With a network of over 1500 industry, government, and community sponsors and partners, the program inspired us to create a K-12 version called RWC (Real World Connections) 17 years ago. We aimed to provide an exciting and engaging STEM learning experience that motivates students in a unique way.

Most people thought it would be limited by traditional course constraints, budget, capacity, politics, students’ capabilities and resources. While they did not see anything but constraints and challenges, we saw nothing but endless possibilities for middle and high school students, university and community.

## Facts About RWC

![](images/about-screenshot-2024-02-17-at-12-16-13-pm.png)

## Our Story

As a network of networks, the multidisciplinary RWC program is changing how classrooms operate and redefining how students learn, running a FREE real-world project-based learning environment year round, transforming the way business work with education, impacting K-12 education through students ownership, and creating a pipeline from middle school to industry while partnering with the world to empower our students.

![](images/about-screenshot-2024-02-17-at-12-02-57-pm.png)

## RWC In Action

In summer 2007, RWC students presented their real-world projects at News12 New Jersey TV and were interviewed by Brian Jenks for more than 20 minutes; in 2012 RWC students presented their innovations at the Liberty Science Center.

## Sense of Ownership

Students select their projects, co-design the course, run class as a company, elect their CEO’s, vote for activities and major decisions and get rewarded for constructive criticism.

## Social Intelligence

Students help each other within the team & between teams, teach the class, advise & mentor peers, sit in a u-shape style and enjoy social activities inside and outside the classroom.

![](images/about-screenshot-2024-02-20-at-7-25-32-pm-2.png)

![](images/about-screenshot-2024-02-17-at-12-33-02-pm.png)

## RWC Achievements

Year 2007

In summer 2007, RWC students presented their real-world projects at News12 NJ TV and were interviewed by Brian Jenks for 20+ minutes. In Fall, the founder of RWC won a national award from Carnegie Foundation with a permanent congressional record for innovation in education and NJIT’s- wide excellence in service award.

Year 2008-09

In summer 2008, RWC students presented their Global Microscope project to NASA headquarters in Washington DC and received an appreciation letter from NASA executive director of education. In summer 2008 and 2009, RWC winners received year-long mentorships from top executives at Saint Barnabas Health Care System.

Year 2012-13

In 2012, RWC students presented their Innovations at the Liberty Science Center. In summer 2013 RWC students ran the entire program by themselves as teachers and managers

Current Partnerships

Partnered with Johnson & Johnson for more than seven years and this partnership only continues to grow. Partnered with the Enterprise Development Center at NJIT, Saint Barnabas Medical Center, several educational, humanitarian and non-profit organizations.

### For Students

### “RWC is a place where we can come and we learn not in a school way but in a fun way where we can express ourselves with creativity and exceed our intelligence.”

## Tracks and Courses

Web Development: Explore the essentials of web development by learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Students engage in hands-on projects that cover responsive design, user experience (UX), and backend development, honing their coding skills while collaborating with peers.

![](images/for-students-2210153.png)

![](images/for-students-remove-png-background-1.png)

Game Design: Unleash creativity in game design! Students discover storytelling, character development, and mechanics while using industry-standard tools to craft immersive gaming experiences. This track blends coding, animation, and graphics.

Animation: Enter the vibrant world of animation, where students bring characters and stories to life. With hands-on projects, they learn principles like movement and timing, using software such as Adobe Animate and Blender to create stunning 2D and 3D animations.

![](images/for-students-5272236.png)

![](images/for-students-remove-png-background-3.png)

Intro to Programming: Begin your coding journey by mastering fundamental programming concepts. Through interactive lessons and practical exercises, students work with languages like Python and JavaScript to create their own projects and solve problems.

Robotics: Design, build, and program robots to tackle challenges! Students explore mechanical engineering, electrical circuits, and programming languages like Python and C++. This track emphasizes problem-solving and innovation through engaging hands-on experiences.

![](images/for-students-remove-png-background-4.png)

![](images/for-students-8460433.png)

Cybersecurity: Learn to protect digital systems from cyber threats! Through interactive activities and real-world simulations, students explore encryption, threat detection, and risk assessment, developing critical skills for safeguarding sensitive information.

App Development: Dive into app development and create mobile applications for smartphones and tablets. Through hands-on projects and expert guidance, students learn the fundamentals of coding and design to bring their ideas to life.

![](images/for-students-5738031.png)

![](images/for-students-2503508.png)

Film: Immerse in the art of filmmaking! From script to screen, students learn storytelling, cinematography, and editing through hands-on projects, gaining experience with industry-standard equipment to produce short films, documentaries, or music videos.

PC and Network Building: Explore the world of PC and network building by constructing and configuring computer systems. Students gain hands-on experience with hardware components, troubleshooting, and optimization, learning essential skills for tech enthusiasts.

![](images/for-students-remove-png-background-2.png)

## See how your child can benefit

### Teamwork

The program provides an environment that cultivates teamwork. Through working together, students learn how to communicate effectively, share ideas, and leverage each other’s skills towards a common goal.

Parents can have peace of mind knowing that their children are in a safe environment while participating in our program
